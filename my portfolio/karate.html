Karate Journey

In 2015, at the age of nine, I stepped onto the dojo floor for the first time. What began as curiosity slowly turned into commitment. Training days stacked into months, months into years. Progress was measured not in speed, but in consistency.

Practicing Shotokan Karate demanded patience, repetition, and accountability. In 2022, I stood for my black belt examination. In April 2023, I earned the rank of 1st Dan Black Belt, becoming one of the youngest practitioners in my dojo to do so. The belt marked a milestone, not a finish line.

Competition tested that discipline under pressure. I represented my training at the Open National level, competing officially three times and securing a Silver Medal at the Funakoshi Open National Cup Competition.

While I am not in daily training today, karate remains present. I return to the dojo when I can. The mindset stays sharper than the punches ever were. The journey continues, quieter now, but far from complete.
